## What is the Context Field?

When you generate a resource on MagiTeacher, you provide three inputs:

1. **The Class** — Year Group, Subject, and Topic
2. **The Output** — Resource type, Difficulty, and Length
3. **The Personalisation** — Context (Details / Specifics / Theme)

The first two inputs tell MagiTeacher *what* to generate. The Context field 
tells it *how*.

Context is a free-text box. Leave it blank and MagiTeacher makes sensible 
choices based on the topic alone. Add something specific and the output shifts 
to match exactly what you described.

---

## The Spectrum: Broad to Precise

Think of Context as a dial that runs from broad to precise.

**Broad** — No context or light context. MagiTeacher has creative freedom. 
Good when you're starting a new unit or want fresh ideas.

**Precise** — Detailed context. MagiTeacher produces a focused, ready-to-use 
output tailored to your exact specification. Good when you know exactly 
what you need.

The same Topic, the same Resource type, the same Year Group — but a different 
Context — produces a completely different resource.

## All the Ways You Can Use Context

---

### Leave It Blank
Let MagiTeacher decide. The output will be broad and topic-led — useful when 
you want a starting point without a specific angle in mind.

---

### Add Topic Detail
Sometimes the topic alone is too broad. Adding specific details narrows 
the focus and produces a more useful, targeted output — without needing 
a theme or pedagogical direction.

- "Fractions — focus specifically on equivalent fractions using area models"
- "World War One — focus on life in the trenches and the experience of 
  British soldiers, not the causes"
- "Seasons — focus on autumn and winter only, and how animals adapt"
- "Volcanoes — focus on the human impact on communities, not the 
  physical process"
- "Romeo and Juliet — focus on Act 3 Scene 1 only"

The topic stays the same. The detail in the Context tells MagiTeacher 
exactly which slice of that topic to cover.

---

### Add a Theme
Wrap the lesson content inside a theme your class will find engaging. 
The curriculum content stays the same — the delivery becomes more motivating.

- "Frame this around a Spider-Man mission scenario"
- "Use a Premier League football context throughout"
- "Set this inside a Minecraft world — students are the builders"
- "Frame using a Great British Bake Off competition format"
- "Link to the coronation of a new monarch"

Works especially well for Do Now Starters, Gamification Narratives, 
Role-Play Scripts, and Homework Tasks — but applies to any resource type.

---

### Describe Your Class
Tell MagiTeacher about the specific group you're teaching. This calibrates 
the output to your actual pupils rather than a generic year group.

- "This is a low-ability Year 8 group who struggle with extended writing"
- "My class has several EAL learners — keep vocabulary accessible"
- "High-ability Year 6 — push beyond standard curriculum expectations"
- "Mixed ability Year 9 — include scaffolding and extension in the same resource"
- "This group has significant gaps — keep it foundational"

---

### Give a Pedagogical Direction
Instruct MagiTeacher to apply a specific teaching approach or learning 
science principle.

- "Apply a Concrete-Pictorial-Abstract (CPA) sequence"
- "Use spaced retrieval practice — reference prior topics from this term"
- "Structure around the PEEL paragraph framework"
- "Apply dual coding — pair each concept with a description of a visual"
- "Use Rosenshine's Principles — build in frequent low-stakes checks"

---

### Add Academic or Exam Precision
For KS4 and KS5, align outputs to specific exam board requirements, 
assessment objectives, or mark scheme language.

- "Focus on AO1 recall and AO3 evaluation — use mark scheme language"
- "Align to AQA GCSE Geography Paper 1 — include 4-mark and 6-mark questions"
- "Integrate Tier 3 vocabulary throughout — define each term in context"
- "Include a calculation using the Inverse Square Law and a graph interpretation task"

---

### Go Cross-Curricular
Link the resource to another subject or a wider theme to build connections 
across the curriculum.

- "Link to the history of the Apollo 11 moon landing"
- "Connect to climate change — use real data where possible"
- "Frame the maths problem around a design and technology project"
